Research

I am a computational epidemiologist and statistician, currently at the Chicago Center for HIV Elimination (CCHE) in the Department of Medicine at the University of Chicago. My work combines statistical and mathematical modeling, network science, and empirical data to understand HIV tranmission dynamics, and design intervention to prevent new infections.

My current work at CCHE is focused on men who have sex with men (MSM) on Southside Chicago, and in Southern India. These projects are supervised by mentor Dr. John Schneider. Currently, I am collaborating with folks at the National Opinion Research Center and the Argonne National Laboratory.

Before coming to Chicago, I worked on network modeling of HIV transmission dynamics in several other populations: circular migrants, pregnant women, serodiscordant couples in various regions of Africa, and MSM in the United States.

I completed my PhD in Quantitative Ecology and Resource Management at the University of Washington (UW), under the supervision of Dr. Steven Goodreau. I also did a post-doc in UW's Department of Global Health, guided by Dr. Ruanne Barnabas and Dr. Connie Celum at UW. While at the UW, I was a member of the Network Modeling Group, and the International Clinical Research Center.
